### 1: WHAT IS DATA ANALYTICS?
Data analytics is the process of cleaning, interpreting, and converting information into actionable insights by detecting patterns and trends.

It’s a complex process involving various stages to extract meaningful insights from raw data - like refining raw material into something valuable and usable<sup>1</sup>.

### 2: TYPES OF DATA ANALYTICS
There are 4 core types orgnisations rely one<sup>1</sup>:

* Descriptive - summarises what has already happened using historical data.
* Diagnostic - digs into why something happened by identifying root causes.
* Predictive - uses models to forecast what is likely to happen next.
* Prescriptive - recommends the best course of action based on those predictions.

### 3: TYPICAL TOOLS USED
Most rely on a combination of tools depending on their needs. The best choice depends on your business needs, who will be using the tool, and whether they’re aimed at technical users, non-technical users, or both<sup>2</sup>. Here’s the most widely used:

* **Microsoft Power BI** - transforms raw data into interactive dashboards and visual reports. Ideal for business intelligence teams already in the MS ecosystem.
* **SQL** - foundation of data querying. Used to retrieve and manage data from relational databases across industries like finance and banking.
* **Python** - versatile programming language used for everything from data cleaning to predictive modelling. Powered by libraries like NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib.

### 4: VS DATA SCIENCE
Data analytics focuses on examining existing data to answer specific questions and inform decisions - so it’s more structured and business facing.

Data science goes broader - using programming, machine learning, and statistical modelling to build predictive systems and uncover unknown patterns.

It’s a task that resides under the data science umbrella <sup>3</sup>.
